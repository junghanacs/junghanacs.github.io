## Junghan Kim

Polymath engineer. Digital gardener. Emacs dweller.

His day is simple. He spends most of it at a keyboard — no mouse, no messengers, no social feeds. The only tool is Emacs. His phone stays silent. Three deep work sessions pass, then night comes. He sleeps at 9, wakes at 4. He is a digital minimalist.

He wasn't always this way. Years of failure and restlessness once defined him. What kept him afloat was writing — putting words down made the unbearable bearable. Writing became both healing and a path toward expanding knowledge: from memory tool to creative instrument, a lifelong companion.

He now knows the question that life has been asking. Even the trials were gifts. His struggles are not his alone — they belong to everyone. What he has lived through must be organized, piece by piece. Not just for healing, but to reach a method of knowledge creation that lasts a lifetime.
